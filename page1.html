Historic Folklore

Drawings resembling the griffin have shown up in drawings for several thousands of years. It can be found in drawings from Ancient Iran, Egypt, Greece, Syria, and more.

Iran

First we will discuss a brief history of Iranian mythology. Griffins aren't called griffins, but Shirdal. Shirdal can be translated to Lion-Eagle.

Later, other creatures resembling griffins were found in other folklore. Drawings of creatures with scaly heads and griffin-like bodies were found during the Bronze Age.
